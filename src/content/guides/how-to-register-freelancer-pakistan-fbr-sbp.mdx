Pakistan has over 1.5 million registered freelancers and earns over $400 million annually in freelance foreign exchange. Yet most freelancers operate informally, missing out on significant tax benefits and facing payment restrictions. This guide covers everything you need to go fully legal — and profitable.

## Why Register as a Freelancer?

| Benefit | Details |
|---|---|
| **Tax exemption on exports** | Freelance income treated as IT export — 0.25% final tax (vs 35% for unregistered) |
| **Retain foreign currency** | Keep 35% of earnings in USD/GBP/EUR in a foreign currency account |
| **Legal international transfers** | Receive from Payoneer, Wise, PayPal via Pakistani banks without complications |
| **FBR filer status** | Avoid double withholding tax on property, vehicles, bank profit |
| **PSEB registration perks** | Business registration, networking, government support |

## Step 1: Register with FBR (Get Your NTN)

Your **NTN (National Tax Number)** is your tax identity. Without it, you cannot file returns or claim the IT export tax exemption.

### How to Register

**Option A: Online (Faster)**
1. Go to [iris.fbr.gov.pk](https://iris.fbr.gov.pk)
2. Click **Registration for Unregistered Person**
3. Enter your CNIC number and mobile number
4. An OTP is sent to your registered mobile
5. Fill in your details:
   - Name (as on CNIC)
   - Address
   - Business type: select **Individual**
   - Nature of business: **IT Services / Freelancing**
   - Business activity code: **7400** (Other Professional & Technical Activities)
6. Submit — your NTN is generated instantly and shown on screen
7. Download the **NTN Certificate**

**Option B: At FBR RTO (Tax Office)**
- Visit your nearest Regional Tax Office (RTO)
- Bring CNIC original + copy, recent utility bill, 1 passport photo
- Fill Form 181 — NTN issued same day

## Step 2: Register with PSEB (Pakistan Software Export Board)

PSEB registration is free and gives you official recognition as a freelancer/IT exporter.

### How to Register
1. Go to [pseb.org.pk](https://www.pseb.org.pk) → Registration
2. Create an account with your email
3. Fill in:
   - Personal details (CNIC, name, address)
   - Freelancing platforms you work on (Upwork, Fiverr, Toptal, etc.)
   - Skills/services offered
   - Annual earnings estimate (in USD)
4. Upload: CNIC copy, NTN certificate, bank statement or Payoneer screenshot
5. PSEB reviews and approves within 5–10 working days
6. You receive a **PSEB Certificate** — keep this for bank account opening

## Step 3: Open a Foreign Currency Account (FCA)

SBP allows freelancers to receive international payments and retain up to 35% in foreign currency.

### Best Banks for Freelancer FCAs
- **HBL** — easiest process, Payoneer partnership
- **Bank Alfalah** — good USD account management
- **Meezan Bank** — Islamic option, efficient FCA process
- **MCB** — reliable, good internet banking for FCAs

### Documents Required
- CNIC original + photocopy
- NTN certificate
- PSEB certificate (some banks require, others don't)
- Existing PKR bank account at the same bank
- Proof of freelancing income (Payoneer statement, Upwork earnings report, client invoices)
- Source of funds declaration (bank provides the form)

### Process
1. Visit the branch with all documents
2. Request to open a **Foreign Currency Current Account (USD)**
3. Declare source of funds as freelancing/IT export income
4. Account opens within 1–3 working days
5. Link your Payoneer or Wise account to this bank account

## Step 4: Receive Payments Legally

### Via Payoneer (Most Popular)
1. Create a Payoneer account at payoneer.com
2. In Payoneer: go to **Add Bank Account** → add your Pakistani bank's SWIFT code + FCA account number
3. Withdraw your Payoneer balance to your FCA
4. Bank converts to PKR at SBP interbank rate (keep 35% in USD if desired)

### Via Wise
1. Create Wise account — verify with CNIC and selfie
2. Add your Pakistani bank FCA as withdrawal destination
3. Wise transfers in USD/GBP/EUR directly to your FCA

### Via Direct Bank Wire (for larger clients)
- Give your client your bank's SWIFT code + FCA IBAN
- Client wires directly — fastest and cheapest for amounts above $500
- Your bank may charge a small receiving fee (PKR 500–2,000)

### Via PayPal
- PayPal withdrawals to Pakistan have limitations — use Payoneer as intermediary (PayPal → Payoneer → Bank)

## Step 5: File Your Annual Tax Return

As a registered freelancer with IT export income, you qualify for a **0.25% final tax** on gross export proceeds — one of the lowest tax rates in Pakistan's tax code.

### How to File
1. Log into [iris.fbr.gov.pk](https://iris.fbr.gov.pk)
2. Select **Income Tax Return** for the relevant tax year
3. Under income: declare **Export of Services income**
4. Enter gross receipts in PKR (use your bank statements as reference)
5. Tax calculated at 0.25% of gross proceeds — this is your **final tax liability**
6. Pay via 1-Link or bank challan
7. Submit return before **September 30**

### What to Keep for Records
- All Payoneer/Wise/bank withdrawal statements
- Client invoices or contracts
- PSEB certificate
- FBR NTN certificate
- Bank FCA statements for the full year

## Common Questions

**Can I work on multiple platforms (Upwork + Fiverr + direct clients)?**
Yes. All income from all platforms is pooled and declared as export of IT services income. Keep records for each platform separately.

**Do I need to register a company?**
No. Freelancing as an individual under your own name is completely legal and simpler than forming a company. Register as a company only when annual revenue exceeds PKR 10–15 million or you need to hire employees formally.

**What if my client pays via crypto?**
Cryptocurrency payments are in a legal grey area in Pakistan. Convert to fiat (USD) via a regulated exchange before receiving in your bank. Do not declare crypto directly on FBR returns without consulting a tax advisor.

**Can I use my regular PKR savings account for freelance income?**
Technically yes, but a dedicated FCA is strongly recommended. It avoids questions about large PKR inflows, allows you to hold foreign currency, and clearly documents your export income for FBR purposes.
