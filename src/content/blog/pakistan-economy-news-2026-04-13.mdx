## Gas Supply to Power Sector May Double Amid LNG Shortfall
The Pakistani government is contemplating a significant shift in its gas supply strategy, with plans to double the gas supply to the power sector by the end of the current month. This move is primarily driven by the shortage of liquefied natural gas (LNG) and the need to prevent sharp tariff hikes and widespread loadshedding. According to reports, the government may divert gas from households, compressed natural gas (CNG), and fertiliser sectors to meet the power sector's demands. This decision underscores the complexity of managing the Pakistan economy today, where balancing the needs of different sectors while ensuring a stable power supply is crucial.

The implications of this move are multifaceted. On one hand, it may help in avoiding significant increases in electricity tariffs, which would have otherwise been passed on to consumers, potentially exacerbating inflation in Pakistan. On the other hand, diverting gas from other sectors could lead to shortages in those areas, impacting industries and households alike. The government's challenge is to navigate these competing demands efficiently, ensuring that the overall impact on the Pakistan economy is minimized.

## Recent Financial Figures from EOBI

## Fuel Subsidy Scheme for Commercial Vehicles
### Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Launches Ehsaas Fuel Support Scheme
In response to the rising fuel prices, the government of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa has announced the launch of the Ehsaas Fuel Support Scheme for commercial vehicles. This initiative aims to provide relief to owners and operators of commercial vehicles, who have been affected by the increase in petroleum prices. The scheme is part of a broader set of measures being implemented by federal and provincial governments to mitigate the impact of high fuel costs on various sectors of the economy.

This move is significant for the Pakistan economy today, as it demonstrates the government's efforts to support businesses and individuals affected by external factors such as fuel price fluctuations. By providing subsidies, the government can help in stabilizing the cost of doing business, which in turn can influence inflation in Pakistan and the overall competitiveness of the economy.

## Market Reactions to Failed US-Iran Talks
### Gold Prices Drop, PSX Slumps
