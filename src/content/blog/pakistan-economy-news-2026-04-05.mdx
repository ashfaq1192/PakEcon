## Gulf War Impact on Pakistan Economy
The ongoing Gulf War has begun to take its toll on the Pakistani economy, with 90% of foreign investment in domestic bonds being withdrawn. According to the State Bank of Pakistan (SBP), this significant withdrawal is a result of the increasing intensity of the conflict, which has diminished hopes for an early recovery. The SBP's data reveals that local investments in treasury bills are on the rise, as returns become more attractive to investors. This shift in investment trends is likely to have a significant impact on the Pakistan economy today, with potential implications for inflation, interest rates, and the overall economic growth.

The Gulf War has also led to a surge in global oil prices, which has had a ripple effect on the Pakistani economy. The PSX has endured a turbulent week, with investor sentiment being unsettled by the rising oil prices. The Pakistan economy latest news suggests that the government is taking measures to mitigate the impact of the oil price shock, including reducing petrol prices. However, the implementation of these measures has been patchy, with some fuel stations not adhering to the reduced prices, leading to public anger and vandalism.

## Oil Price Shock and PSX Performance
The PSX has been experiencing a volatile week, with the historic surge in domestic petroleum prices driven by tightening global oil supplies amid escalating tensions in the Middle East. The rising import bills and potential for renewed monetary tightening have unsettled investor sentiment, leading to a decline in the PSX's performance. The business news Pakistan suggests that the PSX's faltering performance is a result of the oil price shock, which has also led to a increase in freight costs and inter-city transport fares.

## Petrol Price Reduction and Public Anger
The government's decision to reduce petrol prices has not been uniformly implemented, leading to public anger and vandalism. In some areas, fuel stations have not adhered to the reduced prices, resulting in violent incidents and damage to property. The Ehsaas Fuel Support Program, launched by the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Chief Minister, aims to provide a Rs2000 monthly petrol subsidy to motorbikers in the province. However, the program's eligibility criteria and implementation remain unclear, leaving many wondering if it will provide sufficient relief to the public.
