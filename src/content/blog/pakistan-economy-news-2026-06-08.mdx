## Ogra Member Steps Down: What Changed and Why
The Oil and Gas Regulatory Authority (Ogra) is facing a crisis after one of its members stepped down amid investigations into pricing differentials. According to reports, the member (oil) resigned after being questioned by the Federal Investigation Agency (FIA) regarding claims of price differentials. This development has sparked concerns about the autonomy of the regulatory authority and the potential impact on the Pakistan economy. The government is now considering deputing civil servants to head Ogra, which could provide legal cover for the appointment. However, this move may also raise questions about the independence of the regulator.

The pricing probe and subsequent resignation of the Ogra member have significant implications for the oil and gas sector in Pakistan. The country is heavily reliant on imported fuels, and any disruptions to the supply chain can have far-reaching consequences for the economy. The government's decision to depute civil servants to head Ogra may be seen as an attempt to exert greater control over the regulator, but it also raises concerns about the potential for political interference in the decision-making process.

## FBR to Target Non-Filers on Social Media
### Crackdown on Tax Evasion

The FBR will use social media platforms to identify individuals who are not filing tax returns, despite having a visible source of income. This initiative is expected to help the government to broaden the tax base and reduce the burden on honest taxpayers.

The FBR's decision to target non-filers on social media is a significant development in the government's efforts to combat tax evasion. The FBR will need to ensure that its actions are proportionate and do not infringe on the rights of individuals.
