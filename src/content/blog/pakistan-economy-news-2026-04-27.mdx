## KPT Storage Charge Waivers: What Changed and Why
The Federal Minister for Maritime Affairs, Muhammad Junaid Anwar Chaudhry, has announced significant storage charge waivers at Karachi Port Trust (KPT) terminals. The waivers, ranging from 25 to 50%, are intended to provide relief to exporters who have been affected by recent disruptions in Gulf-bound shipments. This decision comes after consultations with terminal operators to address the concerns of exporters. The move is expected to facilitate the export sector, which has been facing challenges due to various disruptions. According to the minister, the storage charge waivers will help reduce the burden on exporters and enable them to compete more effectively in the global market.

The decision to announce storage charge waivers is a positive step towards supporting the export sector, which is a critical component of Pakistan's economy. The country's external sector has been facing challenges due to a large trade deficit, which has been partly offset by workers' remittances. In FY25, workers' remittances reached over $38 billion, which is a significant increase from previous years. The remittances have evolved into a stabilizing force for Pakistan's external sector, effectively anchoring both the current account and broader balance of payments.
