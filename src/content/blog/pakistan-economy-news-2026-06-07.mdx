## IT Export Surge
### Key Takeaways
* Pakistan's services exports grew by 17.68% during the first 10 months of 2025-26
* The information technology sector drove the surge, with earnings reaching $8.27bn
* This growth contrasts with mixed trends in goods exports
* The expansion in services exports is a positive sign for the Pakistan economy

## Services Export Growth: A Positive Sign for Pakistan
The latest data from the government indicates that Pakistan's services exports have experienced a significant surge, driven primarily by the information technology sector. According to the data, services exports grew by 17.68% during the first 10 months of 2025-26, reaching a total of $8.27bn. This growth is a positive sign for the Pakistan economy, which has been facing challenges in recent years. The steady expansion in services exports since the start of FY26 is a welcome development, especially when compared to the mixed trends in goods exports. The government has been working to promote the IT sector, and the results are starting to show. The growth in services exports is expected to contribute positively to the country's balance of payments and help stabilize the PKR exchange rate.

## Opposition to Import Duty Cuts
The ceramic tiles and glass industries have expressed concern over proposed duty cuts on imports of ceramic tiles and glass products. The All Pakistan Ceramic Tiles Manufacturers Association (APCTMA) has stated that the duty cuts would harm the local industry, which is already facing challenges due to high production costs and competition from imported products. The association has urged the government to reconsider the proposed duty cuts and instead focus on providing support to the local industry. This development highlights the challenges faced by the government in balancing the interests of different industries and sectors. On one hand, reducing import duties can help reduce costs for consumers and make Pakistani products more competitive in the international market. On the other hand, it can also harm local industries that are struggling to compete with imported products.

## Solar Wheeling Policy
The government has introduced a solar wheeling policy, which allows consumers to generate their own electricity through solar panels and sell any excess to the grid. This policy is expected to help promote the use of renewable energy in Pakistan and reduce the country's reliance on fossil fuels. The solar wheeling policy is also expected to help reduce electricity costs for consumers, especially those who have access to solar panels. To take advantage of this policy, consumers can install solar panels on their rooftops and generate their own electricity. Any excess electricity can be sold to the grid, providing a source of income for the consumer.
