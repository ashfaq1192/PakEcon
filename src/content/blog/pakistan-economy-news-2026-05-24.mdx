## Cooling Appliances Demand: What's Driving the Surge
The demand for cooling appliances such as refrigerators and deep freezers has surged in the run-up to Eidul Azha, despite the rising cost of living in Pakistan. According to Sohail Malik, finance secretary of the Karachi Electronic Dealers Association (KEDA), the peak summer season is creating extra demand for cooling gadgets. However, dealers claim that prices remain the same as last year, which is a welcome relief for consumers. This surge in demand is likely to benefit the economy, as it will lead to increased sales and revenue for manufacturers and retailers.

The rise in demand for cooling appliances is also an indication of the growing middle class in Pakistan, who are willing to spend on consumer durables. This trend is expected to continue, driven by increasing urbanization and a growing economy. However, the government needs to ensure that the prices of these appliances remain affordable for the common man, by controlling inflation and maintaining a stable economy.

## Rejection of Government's Power Plan
The FPCCI has rejected the government's Indicative Generation Capacity Expansion Plan (IGCEP) 2025-35, submitting its observations to the National Electric Power Regulatory Authority (Nepra) following a public hearing on May 20. The FPCCI has objected to the PLEXOS costing methodology underpinned in the plan, citing concerns over the accuracy of the methodology. This rejection is likely to have significant implications for the Pakistan economy, as it may lead to a revision of the government's power plan and potentially affect the country's energy sector.

The rejection of the government's power plan is also a reflection of the growing concerns over the country's energy sector. The FPCCI has raised important questions about the methodology used to determine the cost of power generation, which is a critical component of the plan. The government needs to address these concerns and ensure that the plan is based on accurate and reliable data.
