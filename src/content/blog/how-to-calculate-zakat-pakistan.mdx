## Introduction
Zakat is one of the five pillars of Islam and is a fundamental aspect of a Muslim's faith. It is a charitable giving to the poor and needy, and its calculation and payment are obligatory for all eligible Muslims. In Pakistan, Zakat calculation and payment are governed by the Zakat and Ushr Ordinance, 1980. To fulfill this obligation correctly, it is essential to understand what Zakat is, who must pay it, and how to calculate it. The nisab threshold, which is the minimum amount of wealth a person must have to be eligible to pay Zakat, is a crucial factor in this calculation. As per the latest OGRA notification and current rates, the nisab threshold is determined by the value of gold and silver. ## Understanding Nisab and Zakat Calculation
The nisab threshold is the minimum amount of wealth a person must have to be eligible to pay Zakat. In Pakistan, the nisab threshold is determined by the value of 7.5 tolas of gold or 52.5 tolas of silver. To calculate the nisab value in PKR, you can use the current market value of gold and silver. As of 2026, the nisab value in PKR can be calculated using the [Gold Price Calculator](https://hisaabkar.pk/tools/gold-price-calculator-pakistan/). Once you have determined the nisab value, you can calculate Zakat on your cash, gold, silver, business assets, and property. For cash and gold, the Zakat rate is 2.5% of the total amount. For example, if you have PKR 100,000 in cash, you would pay PKR 2,500 as Zakat. You can use the [Zakat Calculator](https://hisaabkar.pk/tools/zakat-calculator/) to simplify the calculation process.
