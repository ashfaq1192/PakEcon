## National Economic Council Trims Uplift Plans for FY26-27
The National Economic Council (NEC) has decided to trim uplift plans for the fiscal year 2026-27, with Punjab taking the biggest hit. According to a report by Dawn Business, the NEC has reduced the development budget for Punjab, which will have a significant impact on the province's economy. This decision is expected to affect the overall growth of the Pakistan economy, as Punjab is the largest province in terms of population and economic contribution. The reduction in development budget will likely lead to a decrease in infrastructure development, job creation, and overall economic activity in the province.

The NEC's decision to trim uplift plans is a result of the country's financial constraints and the need to prioritize spending. The government has to balance its budget and allocate resources efficiently to ensure that the most critical sectors are funded. However, this decision may have far-reaching consequences for the people of Punjab, who may face a decline in living standards and economic opportunities. The SBP policy rate and inflation in Pakistan will also be affected by this decision, as the reduced development budget may lead to lower economic growth and higher inflation.

The reduction in income tax will increase disposable income, which can be spent on consumption, savings, or investment, thereby boosting economic activity.
