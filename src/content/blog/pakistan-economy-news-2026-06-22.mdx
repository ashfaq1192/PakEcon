## APTMA and FBR Disagree on Monitoring Cameras
The All Pakistan Textile Mills Association (APTMA) and the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) are at loggerheads over the installation of monitoring cameras. APTMA has opposed the installation of cameras, citing concerns about the impact on the industry. The FBR, on the other hand, has argued that the cameras are necessary to monitor the industry and prevent tax evasion. This dispute is a concern for the Pakistan economy, as the textile industry is a significant contributor to the country's exports.

The dispute between APTMA and FBR is a reflection of the broader challenges facing the Pakistan economy. The country's tax system is complex and often cumbersome, leading to disputes between the government and industries. The installation of monitoring cameras is seen as a way to improve tax compliance, but it is also seen as an invasion of privacy by some industries. The government needs to find a balance between improving tax compliance and protecting the rights of industries.
