## NA, Senate Budget Sessions: What Changed and Why
The President of Pakistan, Asif Ali Zardari, has summoned separate sessions of the National Assembly and the Senate on June 5 to discuss the federal budget for fiscal year 2026-27. The National Assembly session is scheduled to start at 5 pm, while the Senate session will begin at 6 pm. This development is significant as it marks the beginning of the budget-making process for the upcoming fiscal year. The federal budget is a comprehensive document that outlines the government's spending and revenue plans for the year. It is crucial for the Pakistan economy as it will have a direct impact on various sectors, including taxation, healthcare, education, and infrastructure development.

The budget sessions are also an opportunity for lawmakers to debate and discuss the government's economic policies and plans. The sessions will provide a platform for the government to present its vision for the economy and for the opposition to raise their concerns and suggestions. The meetings of the Annual Plan Coordination Committee will also take place, which will help to finalize the budget allocations for various sectors.

The Pakistan economy is currently facing several challenges, including high inflation, a large trade deficit, and a declining PKR exchange rate. The government will need to present a budget that addresses these challenges and provides a clear roadmap for economic growth and development. The budget should also prioritize the needs of the common man, including affordable healthcare, education, and employment opportunities.
