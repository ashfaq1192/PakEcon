## Fuel Price Hike: What Changed and Why
The recent fuel price hike, which has seen petrol and diesel prices rise to Rs400 per litre, is a direct result of the government passing on the global impact of rising fuel costs to consumers. The Rs6.59 increase in petrol prices and the Rs19.39 hike in diesel prices have been implemented with the concurrence of the International Monetary Fund (IMF). This move is likely to have a significant impact on the Pakistan economy today, as it will lead to increased costs for consumers and businesses alike. The SBP news and Pakistan finance news have been closely watching the developments in the fuel sector, as they have a direct impact on inflation Pakistan.

The decision to raise fuel prices has been taken in order to meet the country's petroleum levy target of Rs1.47 trillion. Despite the potential fallout from the Middle East crisis, which could result in a loss of $10-68 billion, the government is confident that it will be able to meet its target. The extension of fuel subsidies for motorcyclists and the transport sector by a month is a welcome move, as it will provide some relief to these groups. However, the overall impact of the fuel price hike will still be felt across the economy.

## FBR Misses Target
### FBR Revenue Shortfall
The Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) has missed its collection target by a significant amount, with a shortfall of Rs684 billion during the first 10 months of the current fiscal year. This is a concerning development, as it will have a direct impact on the government's ability to meet its revenue targets. The FBR has attributed the shortfall to disruptions linked to the ongoing Middle East conflict, which has affected trade and economic activity in the region.

The widening gap between the target and actual collections has become more pronounced over the past two months, and it remains to be seen how the FBR will make up for the shortfall. The government will need to take measures to increase revenue collection, such as improving tax compliance and cracking down on tax evasion. This will be crucial in ensuring that the Pakistan economy today remains on track, despite the challenges posed by the global economic situation.
