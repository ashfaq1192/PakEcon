## Bringing Informal Economy into Tax Net
Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif has announced that the government is taking measures to bring the informal economy into the tax net in the upcoming budget. This declaration was made during a meeting with renowned businessmen and industrialists, where the premier exchanged views on the pace of the country's economic growth. The move is expected to increase tax revenue and reduce the country's reliance on indirect taxes. According to the Prime Minister's Office, the government is committed to implementing policies that promote economic growth and stability. The Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) will play a crucial role in implementing these measures, and it is expected that the FBR will take steps to simplify the tax filing process and reduce the burden on taxpayers.

The informal economy is a significant sector in Pakistan, and bringing it into the tax net could have a substantial impact on the country's economy. The government's efforts to formalize the economy may lead to increased economic growth, as more businesses and individuals will be encouraged to operate within the formal sector. This, in turn, could lead to increased investment, job creation, and economic development. The State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) has also been working to promote financial inclusion and reduce the size of the informal economy. The SBP has introduced various initiatives, such as the Asaan Account, to encourage people to open bank accounts and access formal financial services.

The budget is expected to focus on promoting economic growth, reducing inflation, and increasing tax revenue. The Oil and Gas Regulatory Authority (OGRA) has also been working to reduce the burden of high energy prices on consumers.
