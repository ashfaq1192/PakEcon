## FBR Beats Tax Target
### Key Takeaways
* The Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) has collected over Rs13 trillion in FY26, exceeding the revised target by Rs21 billion.
* The target was achieved mainly due to stronger-than-expected income tax receipts, particularly in June.
* The FBR's success in meeting its tax target is a positive development for the Pakistan economy.
* The government's strategy to shift to a Riba-free financial system by 2027 is underway, with new government loans to be Shariah-compliant from 2028.

## FBR Achieves Tax Target: What Changed and Why
The Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) has announced that it has collected over Rs13 trillion in FY26, exceeding the downwardly revised target of Rs12.983 trillion by Rs21 billion. This achievement is largely attributed to higher-than-anticipated income tax collection in June. The provisional revenue also exceeded the expected amount, demonstrating the FBR's efforts to improve tax collection and broaden the tax base. This success is a significant development for the Pakistan economy, as it indicates the government's ability to meet its revenue targets and implement effective tax policies.

The FBR's achievement is a result of its efforts to strengthen tax enforcement, improve taxpayer services, and enhance transparency. The board has implemented various measures to increase tax compliance, including the introduction of a new tax return form and the expansion of the tax net to include more individuals and businesses. Additionally, the FBR has taken steps to reduce tax evasion and avoidance, such as increasing audits and inspections, and implementing a system for tracking and monitoring tax payments.

## New Government Loans to be Shariah-Compliant
The Ministry of Finance has drafted a strategy for a post-2027 Riba-free financial system, which includes making new government loans Shariah-compliant from 2028. Existing conventional loans will continue until maturity, and majority foreign-owned banks may offer both Islamic and conventional products. This move is part of the government's efforts to shift towards a more Islamic financial system, which is expected to have a positive impact on the economy.

The introduction of Shariah-compliant loans is expected to increase financial inclusion and provide more options for individuals and businesses. It will also help to reduce the country's reliance on conventional banking and promote a more equitable and just financial system. The government's strategy is a significant step towards achieving its goal of a Riba-free financial system by 2027, and it is expected to have a positive impact on the economy in the long run.
