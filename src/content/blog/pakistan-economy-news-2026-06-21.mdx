## PSX Bullish Week: What Changed and Why
The Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX) maintained its bullish momentum during the outgoing week, with the benchmark KSE-100 index briefly crossing the 180,000 mark for the first time since February. This surge in the stock market can be attributed to peace and budget optimism. However, the index failed to sustain this level and retreated in the final session of the week on renewed geopolitical concerns in the Middle East. The PSX's performance is a significant indicator of the Pakistan economy, and this bullish week is a positive sign for investors. According to the Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan (SECP), the country's stock market has been performing well despite various challenges.

The KSE-100 index is a key benchmark for the PSX, and its performance has a significant impact on the overall economy. The index's brief crossing of the 180,000 mark is a testament to the market's resilience and the investors' confidence in the Pakistan economy. The State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) has also played a crucial role in maintaining economic stability, and its monetary policy decisions have a direct impact on the stock market. The SBP's policy rate, currently at 17.5%, has been effective in controlling inflation, which has been a major concern for the economy.

## Climate Action and Budget Allocation
Climate action has taken a backseat in the federal budget FY27, with allocations in all climate heads facing cuts, except for disaster management. This move has been criticized by experts, who call for transparency in climate spending and structural reforms. The lack of focus on climate action is a concern, given the country's vulnerability to climate change. The Pakistan economy is heavily dependent on agriculture, which is directly affected by climate change. The Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) has also been working to improve the tax collection system, which is essential for funding climate-related initiatives.

The climate allocations in the federal budget FY27 are a disappointment, considering the country's commitment to reducing greenhouse gas emissions. The government needs to prioritize climate action and allocate sufficient funds to address the challenges posed by climate change. The National Electric Power Regulatory Authority (NEPRA) has also been working to promote renewable energy sources, which is essential for reducing the country's carbon footprint.

## Mango Exports and Gas Pipeline Project
Pakistan's mango exports have shrunk due to the lingering impact of the Middle East war. The country's mango industry is a significant contributor to the economy, and the decline in exports is a concern. The government needs to take measures to support the industry and find alternative markets for mango exports. The Oil and Gas Regulatory Authority (OGRA) has also been working to improve the energy sector, which is essential for the economy.

The Pak-Iran gas pipeline project remains under consideration, according to Ali Pervaiz Malik. The project has been in the pipeline for several years, and its completion is essential for addressing the country's energy needs. The project will not only provide a reliable source of energy but also promote economic cooperation between Pakistan and Iran. The Pakistan Bureau of Statistics (PBS) has also been working to improve the data collection system, which is essential for making informed economic decisions.
