## Govt Hybrid Plan: Power Crisis
### Key Takeaways

* LNG supply is expected to drop to near zero from next month, and gas supply to the power sector will drop sharply from April

## Govt Hybrid Plan: Power Crisis Details
The Pakistani government has come up with a hybrid plan to tackle the ongoing power crisis, which includes a combination of load-shedding and tariff hikes. According to reports, the average citizen can expect two to three hours of daily load-shedding, which will undoubtedly have a significant impact on daily life and business operations. Furthermore, the government is planning to increase electricity tariffs, which will add to the financial burden of households and businesses already struggling with high energy costs. The situation is expected to worsen from next month, with LNG supply dropping to near zero and gas supply to the power sector decreasing sharply from April. The railways dispute is also affecting coal transport to power plants, which is exacerbating the crisis.

## Global Oil Price Surge
### Impact on Pakistan
The recent surge in global oil prices, driven by the escalating conflict in the Middle East, is likely to have a significant impact on the Pakistani economy. With Brent crude heading for a record monthly rise, Pakistan's oil import bill is expected to increase, putting pressure on the country's foreign exchange reserves and potentially leading to a depreciation of the PKR exchange rate. This, in turn, could lead to higher inflation, as the cost of imported goods increases. The government may need to take measures to mitigate the impact of rising oil prices, such as increasing fuel prices or implementing energy-saving measures.

## South Korea's Vehicle Rotation System
### A Possible Solution for Pakistan?
South Korea's Finance Minister has hinted at expanding the country's mandatory five-day vehicle rotation system to the private sector if global oil prices rise to $120-130 per barrel. This move is part of the country's nationwide energy-saving efforts amid the escalating conflict in the Middle East. Pakistan could potentially learn from South Korea's example and implement similar measures to reduce its own oil consumption and mitigate the impact of rising global oil prices. By promoting the use of public transport, carpooling, and other energy-efficient practices, Pakistan could reduce its reliance on imported oil and decrease its vulnerability to price shocks.

## Gold and Dollar Market Trends
### Impact on Pakistan's Economy
The recent decline in gold prices, which has lost over 15% this month, may have implications for Pakistan's economy. Gold is often seen as a safe-haven asset, and its decline may indicate a decrease in investor risk appetite. On the other hand, the US dollar has held firm despite the rising risk of a protracted Middle East war, which could lead to a decrease in investor sentiment and potentially impact Pakistan's economy. The State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) may need to monitor these developments closely and adjust its monetary policy accordingly to maintain economic stability.
