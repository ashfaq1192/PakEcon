## Understanding SBP Interbank Rate and Open Market Difference
The SBP interbank rate is the rate at which banks and other financial institutions buy and sell foreign currencies among themselves. This rate is determined by the SBP through a mechanism that takes into account various economic factors, including inflation, interest rates, and trade balances. ## Impact of IMF Program on PKR and Inflation
The International Monetary Fund (IMF) program has had a significant impact on the PKR exchange rate in recent years. As part of the program, Pakistan has implemented various economic reforms, including a flexible exchange rate regime. This has led to a weakening of the PKR against the USD, making imports more expensive and contributing to higher inflation. The current rates of inflation in Pakistan are a concern for many, and the weakening PKR has made it essential for individuals and businesses to manage their foreign exchange transactions carefully. The Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) and the Ministry of Finance are working together to implement policies that will help stabilize the PKR exchange rate and reduce inflation. The National Electric Power Regulatory Authority (NEPRA) and the Oil and Gas Regulatory Authority (OGRA) are also playing a crucial role in regulating the prices of essential commodities, which are affected by the exchange rate.
