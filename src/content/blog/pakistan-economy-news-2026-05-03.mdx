## Oil Spike Threatens Pakistan's Economic Growth
The recent surge in oil prices poses a significant threat to Pakistan's economic growth, with analysts warning that inflation may exceed 11% if the trend persists. The unresolved Middle East conflict has led to a sharp increase in oil prices, which is likely to have a ripple effect on the country's economy. According to experts, the GDP expansion may slow to 2.5-3.0% in FY27, which is a significant decline from the previous year. The rising costs and disrupted imports are major concerns for the economy, and the government will need to take measures to mitigate the impact of the oil price surge.

The SBP, which has been closely monitoring the situation, may need to revisit its monetary policy to address the emerging challenges. The PKR exchange rate, which has been under pressure in recent months, may come under further stress if the oil prices continue to rise. The inflation Pakistan has been a major concern for the government, and the latest developments are likely to make it even more challenging to control prices. The business news Pakistan has been dominated by the oil price surge, and the impact on the economy will be closely watched by stakeholders.

## TradeBoosting Efforts
