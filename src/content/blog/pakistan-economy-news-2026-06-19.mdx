## PSX Tumbles: What Changed and Why
The Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX) benchmark KSE-100 index experienced a significant decline, plummeting by over 2,800 points during intraday trade. This downturn can be attributed to the uncertainty surrounding US-Iran talks, which have led to falling oil prices. As reported by Dawn Business, the PSX's four-day gaining spree was broken, resulting in a substantial loss for investors. The decline in oil prices has been a major contributor to the decline in the PSX, as it has a significant impact on the country's economy. The PSX is closely tied to the performance of the oil and gas sector, and any fluctuations in oil prices can have a ripple effect on the entire market.

The uncertainty over US-Iran talks has created a sense of unease among investors, leading to a decline in investor confidence. This, in turn, has resulted in a decrease in investment in the PSX, contributing to the decline in the market. The PSX's decline is a reflection of the broader economic trends in Pakistan, which have been affected by the country's struggle to attract foreign investment. According to a report by Dawn Business, profit repatriation exceeded inflows of foreign direct investment (FDI) by 32 per cent in the first 11 months of 2025-26. This highlights the weak appeal of Pakistan's economy for fresh capital and the challenges faced by the country in keeping the current account under control.
