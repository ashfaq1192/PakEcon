## IMF Request for Fuel Price Reforms: What Changed and Why
The International Monetary Fund (IMF) has recently asked Pakistan to eliminate distortions in petroleum pricing as soon as possible. This request comes despite the IMF's tacit acceptance of the Rs152 billion subsidy cap extended by the federal government to consumers. The subsidy cap was introduced in response to a historic global price surge following the US-Israeli attacks on Iran. The IMF's request highlights the need for Pakistan to address the underlying issues in its fuel pricing mechanism, which has been affected by the ongoing subsidy pressures. According to the IMF, removing fuel price distortions will help Pakistan to reduce its fiscal burden and improve the overall efficiency of its economy.

The IMF's request is also driven by concerns about the impact of fuel subsidies on Pakistan's fiscal deficit. The subsidies have been eating into the government's budget, making it challenging for Pakistan to meet its fiscal targets. By removing the distortions in fuel pricing, Pakistan can reduce its subsidy bill and allocate more resources to other critical areas of the economy. The IMF's request is in line with its efforts to support Pakistan's economic reform program, which aims to stabilize the country's economy and promote sustainable growth.

## Oil Price Rise and OPEC+ Meeting
The ongoing US-Israeli war with Iran has disrupted oil supplies, leading to a rise in oil prices. Brent crude futures have increased by $1.71, or 1.6%, to $110.74 a barrel, while US West Texas Intermediate crude futures have gained $0.71, or 0.6%, to trade at $105.45 a barrel. The rise in oil prices is likely to affect Pakistan's economy, as the country is heavily reliant on imported oil. The OPEC+ oil cartel will discuss production quotas for next month, considering the impact of the Iran war on global oil markets. The meeting will be crucial in determining the direction of oil prices in the coming months.
