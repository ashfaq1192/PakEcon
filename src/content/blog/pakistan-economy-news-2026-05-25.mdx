## PSX Bullish Note: What Changed and Why
The Pakistan Stock Exchange's (PSX) benchmark index began the week on a strong note, gaining 2,800 points during early trading. According to Dawn Business, the KSE-100 index had gained 3,594 points and was at 171,438 points at 9:30am, compared to Friday's close of 167,844.24 points. By 9:39am, the index had slightly dipped to 170,714 points, up by 2,870 points. This surge in the stock market is a positive sign for the Pakistan economy, indicating investor confidence in the country's economic prospects. The development comes amid hopes that the economy is recovering from the recent challenges, including high inflation and a large trade deficit.

## Cotton Imports and Oil Price Fall
### Cotton Shortage Hits Pakistan
Pakistan is facing a severe cotton shortage, with local textile mills importing cotton from the US and Brazil even before the start of the new cotton ginning season. According to Dawn Business, local textile mills have purchased an extraordinary 206,000 bales of cotton from the US and Brazil, triggering a sharp surge in domestic cotton and phutti prices. This shortage is likely to impact the textile industry, which is a significant contributor to Pakistan's economy.

### Oil Prices Fall to Two-Week Lows
Oil prices have fallen to two-week lows on hopes of a US-Iran Hormuz deal, according to ARY Business. This decline in oil prices is likely to have a positive impact on Pakistan's economy, as the country is a significant importer of oil. Lower oil prices will help reduce the country's oil import bill, which will have a positive impact on the trade deficit and the overall economy.

## Digital Economy and Housing Fraud
### Pakistan Signs MoUs with Alibaba
Pakistan has signed strategic MoUs with Alibaba to boost its digital economy, according to ARY Business. This partnership is expected to help Pakistan develop its e-commerce sector and increase its exports. The development of the digital economy is critical for Pakistan, as it will help create new job opportunities and increase the country's competitiveness in the global market.

### Prime Suspect in Housing Fraud Arrested
The prime suspect in a multi-billion-rupee Pakistan housing fraud has been arrested in the UAE after an Interpol-led operation, according to Profit Pakistan. The suspect is alleged to have duped at least 295 victims of over Rs1.4 billion in a fake plot scheme. This arrest is a significant development in the case and is expected to provide relief to the victims of the fraud.
